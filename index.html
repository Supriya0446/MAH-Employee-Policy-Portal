Policies: 6

Maharaja Agrasen Hospital is committed to ensuring that employees are treated fairly, provided with appropriate support, and made aware of their rights throughout their employment.

Employees are entitled to:
  • Fair compensation appropriate to the role performed.
  • Clear employment terms and conditions before joining the organization.
  • Protection against unfair dismissal, transfer, or demotion in accordance with internal policy.
  • Leave and statutory benefits as applicable under legal requirements.
  • A safe workplace and healthy working environment.
  • Equal treatment and non-discrimination, irrespective of race, color, sex, or religion.
  • Training, updates, and role-related guidance necessary for effective performance.
  • The right to raise grievances and be informed of disciplinary actions.
  • Privacy in personal matters and a clear resignation and exit process.

Every employee is expected to contribute to a safe, respectful, ethical, and professionally disciplined workplace.

Key Expectations:
  • Maintain punctuality and regular attendance.
  • Wear the prescribed uniform and identity card while on duty.
  • Be familiar with emergency codes, hospital policies, and work instructions.
  • Inform the Supervisor or Head of Department in advance of absence.
  • Notify Human Resources of changes in personal records.
  • Report to work well-groomed, using required safety equipment.
  • Treat colleagues, patients, and visitors with respect.
  • Protect the confidentiality of hospital and patient information.
  • Exercise due care in the use of hospital property.

Any act or omission harmful to patient care delivery, workplace discipline, or the reputation of the Hospital may be treated as misconduct.

Definition of Misconduct:

Includes any act detrimental to healthcare services, discipline, or the prestige of Maharaja Agrasen Hospital. Specific acts are detailed in the Service Rule Book.

Enquiry Process:
  • Management may appoint an Enquiry Officer for serious cases.
  • The employee will be informed of the date, time, and place of the enquiry.
  • The employee will be given a full opportunity to respond to the charges.
  • Defense may be provided by a co-employee from the same department (under specific conditions).
  • No outsiders are authorized to assist or represent the employee.

A formal mechanism to ensure concerns are heard, reviewed fairly, and resolved in a timely and structured manner.

Grievance Escalation Process:
  • Step 1: Submit the grievance to the Head of Department (HOD).
  • Step 2: If unsatisfied, escalate the matter to the Manager - HR.
  • Step 3: If unresolved, approach the Senior Administrative Officer / Medical Superintendent.
  • Step 4: Final appeal to the Medical Director, who may constitute a Grievance Redressal Committee.

Note: All employees are encouraged to use these channels to address concerns constructively and without delay.

Employees who wish to resign must follow the formal exit process established by the Hospital.

Key Requirements:
  • Notice Period: One, two, or three months as applicable to the employment terms.
  • Exit Clearance: Obtain a No Dues Certificate from the Human Resources Department.
  • Property Return: Return identity cards and all organizational property before final separation.
  • Exit Interview: Conducted by the HR Head to gather feedback for organizational improvement.
Other Separations:

Retirement (Superannuation) occurs at age 58 or if declared medically unfit. Involuntary separation is governed by hospital service rules regarding misconduct and disciplinary action.

The Hospital is committed to maintaining a healthy workforce and a safe environment through preventive and operational safety measures.

Safety Protocols:
  • Health Checks: Pre-employment, periodic, and annual assessments.
  • Operational Safety: Fire safety, infection control, and safe handling of dangerous equipment/biomedical waste.
  • Training: Mandatory fire safety training and periodic fire drills.
  • Medical Attention: Immediate care provided for workplace accidents or injuries.

Strict Requirement: The entire hospital premises is a designated no-smoking zone. Employees must comply with all health and safety instructions.

Maharaja Agrasen Hospital maintains a workplace free from sexual harassment, intimidation, and discrimination.

Complaint Mechanism:
  • Written complaints should be submitted to the Sexual Harassment Committee (ICC).
  • The committee ensures the matter is reviewed sensitively and appropriately.
  • Complainants and witnesses are protected against victimization or discrimination.
  • Options such as transfer of the perpetrator or complainant may be examined.

Records: All grievances are officially recorded in a register maintained by the HR Department.

ICC Complaint Flow Reference

For internal employee reference only. Complaints must be submitted in written format as per hospital policy.

Employees may submit a written complaint to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) regarding incidents of workplace sexual harassment.

Process:
  • Submit a written complaint to the Sexual Harassment Committee.
  • The complaint will be reviewed confidentially.
  • The Committee will examine the matter fairly and sensitively.
  • Recommendations will be made in accordance with hospital policy.
  • All complaints are recorded by the HR Department.

The Internal Complaints Committee provides appropriate support to employees raising complaints of workplace sexual harassment.

Support Measures:
  • Protection against victimization or discrimination.
  • Confidential handling of complaints.
  • Sensitive review of all reported incidents.
  • Consideration of transfer of the complainant where appropriate.
  • Consideration of transfer of the alleged perpetrator where appropriate.
  • Maintenance of grievance records by the HR Department.

Every employee shall retire from service in accordance with applicable service conditions of the Hospital.

Retirement Conditions:
  • On attaining the age of 58 years.
  • When declared medically unfit for duty by authorized medical authority.

Further details are governed by the Hospital Service Rule Book.

Termination of employment shall be governed by the Hospital Service Rules.

Applicable Situations:
  • Misconduct or disciplinary action
  • Violation of hospital policies
  • Operational or administrative requirements

All separations will follow due process as per applicable rules and regulations.

The Human Resources Department maintains official employee records securely.

Employee Responsibilities:
  • Ensure accuracy of personal and employment information.
  • Notify HR promptly of any changes in details.
  • Maintain updated documentation as required.

The Hospital ensures secure handling of all patient and organizational data.

Key Principles:
  • Confidentiality and data security must be maintained at all times.
  • Access is restricted to authorized personnel only.
  • Data must not be shared without proper approval.

The Hospital ensures continuity of services during system downtime or failure.

Key Measures:
  • Manual fallback procedures during system outage.
  • Data backup and recovery systems.
  • Staff training for emergency operations.

Hospital data is stored securely in both electronic and physical formats.

Data Management Practices:
  • Secure storage with restricted access.
  • Regular backups and system monitoring.
  • Controlled retention and archival process.

The Hospital ensures complete confidentiality and integrity of medical records.

Core Standards:
  • Secure storage and controlled access.
  • Disclosure only with proper authorization.
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.

All policies are governed by authorized hospital management.

Key Points:
  • Policies are applicable hospital-wide.
  • Changes require approval from authorized authorities.
  • Departments are responsible for awareness and implementation.
